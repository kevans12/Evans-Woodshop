It all starts with the raw lumber. We have a couple different places we source form, depending on the size of board we need.

Fortunately we have several places nearby that sell large boards (for rolling pins) and small pieces (for shaving sets).


Once we have the boards, we cut them down to the right size.

Larger boards are ripped down on the table saw, while smaller ones we'll take over to the band saw.


You can see here, this is what looks like when we've got a board cut down to the right size for rolling pins.

These ones are ready to be put on the lathe and turned into rolling pins.


Once we've cut them down, it's time to get them up on the lathe.

Whether it's a large rolling pin or a small shaving stand, each item is handturned until it is just right.
